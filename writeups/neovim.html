Why i use neovim over other IDEs:

Jan 2026

Neovim is my tiny, furious command center: modal, lightning‑fast, and endlessly scriptable. One tidy init.lua, a curated set of plugins, and my editor behaves like a bespoke instrument that anticipates my bad habits and forgives my typos. Its philosophy of doing one thing well and letting users extend the rest fits my tinkerer brain — I get power without bloat, and the whole setup rewards muscle memory and customization. As for the vim vs emacs circus, I’m firmly in the modal camp but I enjoy the roast: Emacs users claim they’ve built entire universes inside their editor, while I respond with a flurry of keystrokes and a smug plugin; it’s less a war and more a long, affectionate roast where everyone steals the good ideas and pretends they invented them.
